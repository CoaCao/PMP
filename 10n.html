Dear streamlining project team,

The budget change announced yesterday will impact our project. I may need to reprioritize some of our planned work.

Question 5 Answer

b.
Wait for the product owner’s decision.
c.

d.

Feedback

Explanation: Reprioritizing the backlog (A) reflects the agile principle of adapting to change while maintaining momentum. The product owner’s message signals a likely shift in priorities due to budget constraints, but it does not instruct the team to stop work. The team lead should respond proactively by recommending a backlog reprioritization to ensure the remaining iterations focus on the highest-value work under the new budget conditions. This keeps the team productive and aligned with evolving business needs. Option B delays action unnecessarily, Option C steps outside the team lead’s role, and Option D is premature and not aligned with agile practices of responding to change constructively.

A hotel chain wants to improve their customer loyalty program by increasing the number of returning guests by 35%. The project manager hears about two strong stakeholder opinions:

  • Stakeholder J suggests the sales teams focus on the top 10 corporate clients and incentivize them toward exclusive use of this hotel chain.
  • Stakeholder M suggests improving the quality of their airport locations, which already has many returning guests. Stakeholder M also insists the chain cannot compete in larger markets, so the strategy stakeholder J suggests will fail.

In a dramatic escalation, stakeholder M emails the project manager and entire leadership team accusing stakeholder J of bias.

Question 14 Answer

b.
Coach stakeholder M about professional behavior and ask them to apologize to stakeholder J.
c.
Escalate this issue to human resources (HR).
d.

Feedback

Explanation: The problem in this scenario is stakeholder M’s challenging behavior. Trying one more time to determine an initial scope is the best action because it focuses on the solution, not the personal battle; however, if neither coaching nor mediation work, escalation may become the only option. It is likely not within any project manager’s authority to remove stakeholders from projects, even though this would calm the situation.

An agile team working on a project requires a team member with a specialized skill. Because of time limitations, the agile team has two options: borrow a part-time team member from another department or use an independent contractor. The eight team members discuss the options but are unable to reach an agreement. The vote is 50/50, and both options have positive benefits as well as risks.

What should the project team do next?

Question 25 Answer
a.
Review the risk register and choose the option without a secondary risk.
b.
Guide the team through a multicriteria decision analysis.
c.
Wait until the next retrospective and ask again.
d.
Ask the product owner to decide.

Feedback

Explanation: A multicriteria decision analysis (MCDA) is a structured decision-making technique that helps teams evaluate complex options based on defined criteria such as cost, risk, availability, and impact. In this scenario, the team is evenly split between two viable choices, each with pros and cons. Rather than relying on a simple majority or deferring the decision (C), guiding the team through an MCDA allows for an objective, transparent process that supports collaboration and aligns with agile principles. Option A is inappropriate because secondary risks are not the only consideration, and option D undermines team empowerment and shared ownership.

ECO Task 1.4: Engage stakeholders

The correct answer is: Guide the team through a multicriteria decision analysis.
